Summary

The results in this report have been produced using data from the ISARIC COVID-19 database. For information, or to contribute to the collaboration, please contact ncov@isaric.org.

We thank all of the data contributors for collecting standardised data during these extraordinary times. We plan to issue this report of aggregate data regularly for the duration of the SARS-CoV-2/COVID-19 pandemic.

Please note the following caveats. This is a dynamic report which captures new variables and information as our understanding of COVID-19 evolves. Please observe the N of each result to note variables with fewer data points. Information is incomplete for the patients who are still being treated. Furthermore, it is likely that that we received more cases of severely ill individuals than those with relatively less severe illness; outcomes from these data, such as the proportion dying, must therefore not be used to infer outcomes for the entire population of people who might become infected. Some patients may be participants in clinical trials of experimental interventions. Note the variation in patient numbers per country. Additional caveats are provided in the in the ‘Caveats’ section below.

Up to the date of this report, data have been entered for 251407 individuals from 744 sites across 57 countries.

The analysis detailed in this report only includes individuals:

  1. for whom data collection commenced on or before 16 November 2020. (We have applied a 14-day rule to focus analysis on individuals who are more likely to have a recorded outcome. By excluding patients enrolled during the last 14 days, we aim to reduce the number of incomplete data records and thus improve the generalisability of the results and the accuracy of the outcomes;

AND

  1. who have laboratory-confirmed or clinically-diagnosed SARS-COV-2 infection.

The cohort satisfying the above criteria has 251407 cases (210012 are laboratory-confirmed for SARS-COV-2 infection). The below flow chart gives an overview of the cohort and outcomes as of 30 November 2020.

Demographics and presenting features

Of these 251407 cases, 125477 are males and 117468 are females – sex is unreported for 8462 cases. The minimum and maximum observed ages were 0 and 120 years respectively. The median age is 60 years.

The observed mean number of days from (first) symptom onset to hospital admission was 6.1, with a standard deviation (SD) of 8.1 days and a median of 4 days.

The observed mean duration for the number of days from hospital admission to outcome (death or discharge) was 12, with SD 17.2 days and a median of 8 days. These estimates are based on all cases which have complete records on length of hospital stay (N = 205800).

The observed symptoms on admission partly represent case definitions and policies for hospital admission, which may change as time passes. The five most common symptoms at admission were cough, history of fever, shortness of breath, fatigue/malaise, and altered consciousness/confusion. Frequencies of symptom prevalence vary with age. 35440/120606 (29.4%) patients presented with oxygen saturations <94%.

Outcomes

Outcomes have been recorded for 209902 patients, consisting of 161789 recoveries and 48113 deaths. Follow-up is ongoing for 2508 patients. Outcome records are unavailable for 38997 patients.

ICU/HDU: A total of 24382 (10%) patients were admitted at some point of their illness into an intensive care unit (ICU) or high dependency unit (HDU). Of these, 7869 died, 46 are still in hospital and 11054 have recovered and been discharged. Outcome records are unavailable for 5413 cases.

The observed mean and median durations (in days) from hospital admission to ICU/HDU admission were 2.3 and 0 respectively (SD: 9.1) – estimated from records on cases with complete date records on hospital admission and ICU/HDU entry (N = 20684).

The duration of stay in ICU/HDU had a mean of 12.1 days and a median of 7 (SD: 13.7 days) – estimated on only those cases with complete records for ICU/HDU duration or ICU/HDU start/end dates (N = 20122). Approximately 52% of patients with complete records on ICU admission dates were admitted to ICU within the first day of hospital admission. Treatment

Antibiotics were received by 89833/111899 (80.3%) patients, and 15980/111543 (14.3%) received antivirals. These treatment categories are not mutually exclusive since some patients received multiple treatments. (The denominators differ due to data completeness.) 77519/128426 (60.4%) patients received some degree of oxygen supplementation: of these, 17570/123878 (14.2%) received NIV and 15537/124858 (12.4%) IMV.

Of the patients admitted into ICU/HDU, 18825/20115 (93.6%) received antibiotics and 6548/19972 (32.8%) antivirals. 20050/21158 (94.8%) received some degree of oxygen supplementation, of which, 9447/20969 (45.1%) received NIV and 13193/21078 (62.6%) IMV.

A total of 17570 patients received non-invasive mechanical ventilation (NIV). The mean and median durations from admission to receiving NIV were 13.3 days and 9 days respectively (SD: 14.2 days) – estimated from records on cases with complete records on dates of hospital admission and NIV onset (N = 16516). The mean and median durations for NIV were 8.2 days and 4 days respectively (SD: 13.1 days) – estimated based on only those cases which have complete NIV duration records (N = 17386).

A total of 15537 patients received invasive mechanical ventilation (IMV). The mean and median durations from admission to receiving IMV were 12.4 days and 10 days respectively (SD: 12.4 days) – estimated from records on cases with complete records on dates of hospital admission and IMV onset (N = 13796). The mean, median and SD for the duration of IMV – estimated based on all 15238 cases with complete records on IMV stays – were 14.4 days, 7 days and 19.8 days respectively.

Corticosteroids were administered to 29874/117128 (25.5%) patients. This includes 6935/14212 (48.8%) of those who received IMV, 16962/56972 (29.8%) of those who had oxygen therapy but not IMV, and 5184/42617 (12.2%) of those who had no oxygen therapy. On 16 June, results for dexamethasone were released for the RECOVERY randomized controlled trial (RECOVERY, 2020; RECOVERY Collaborative Group, 2020). This trial found that dexamethasone reduced deaths for patients receiving IMV and oxygen therapy, but not among patients not receiving respiratory support. Of patients admitted since 16 June, corticosteroids were received by 2326/3092 (75.2%) of those who received IMV, 8462/10978 (77.1%) of those who had oxygen therapy but not IMV, and 2260/9922 (22.8%) of those who had no oxygen therapy.
